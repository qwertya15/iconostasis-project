Overview

The Iconostasis, translated as Icon screen, is typically made of wood, and is one of the most important architectural features of the Orthodox Church. It separates the Sanctuary, where the Lord is permanently present, from the nave of the church, where worshippers assemble seeking the divine life. In doing so, it represents the gates of Heaven. The various icons hanging on the Iconostasis declare the reconciliation between the two worlds, the heavenly and the earthly, establishing that all previous separation has been overcome through the Cross.
